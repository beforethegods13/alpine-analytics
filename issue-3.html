The Guest House · Truckee, California

The Guest House

Tucked into the pines in Truckee, The Guest House is less a rental and more a fully serviced private stay. A house captain runs point, a private chef keeps things dialed, and a Rivian SUV with driver stands ready for mountain laps or dinner runs.

We came up with friends — Matt and Madie. Matt, freshly retired from Major League Baseball, clicked into skis for the first time.

The Guest House proved the perfect basecamp: equal parts confidence and recovery lounge.

Sundance Inn · Provo Canyon, Utah

Sundance Inn

Sundance Inn at dusk
(drop in: sundance-inn.jpg)
"Bob's Bighorn Sheep"
above the hearth
(sundance-bighorn.jpg)

Most people hear "Sundance" and think film festival. Fewer know it started here, in Provo Canyon, when Robert Redford began shaping this resort in the late 1960s — built not for spectacle, but for art, land, and a slower kind of luxury.

The new Sundance Inn leans into that legacy. Timbered, grounded, and quietly refined, it favors atmosphere over flash — rooms that open to the mountain, design that says out of the way, and a rhythm that feels intentionally unhurried.

Inside, the details land softly. In the lounge, "Bob's Bighorn Sheep" — by Utah artist Daniel Bailey — is mounted above the stone hearth. Fitting, given Redford's role in reintroducing bighorn sheep to the area: a nod to the West, equal parts story and signature.

Robert Redford built Sundance for moments like this. The secret's still intact — let's keep it that way.
